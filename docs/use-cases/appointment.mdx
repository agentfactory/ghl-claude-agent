# Use Case: Book an Appointment

This prompt tells Claude to check your GHL calendar for availability, respond to a booking request, and create the appointment.

**Time saved:** 3 to 5 minutes per booking, and you never have to switch to the calendar tab.

## What This Does

1. Claude checks your GHL calendar for open time slots
2. Identifies available windows based on your preferences
3. Books the appointment on the calendar
4. Optionally notifies the contact with the confirmed time

## The Prompt

Copy this prompt and paste it into Claude. Replace the placeholder values.

```
Check my GoHighLevel calendar [CALENDAR NAME] for available
time slots this week.

I need to book a [DURATION]-minute meeting with the contact
[CONTACT NAME].

Preferences:
- Preferred days: [e.g., Tuesday through Thursday]
- Preferred times: [e.g., afternoon, between 1pm and 4pm]
- Timezone: [YOUR TIMEZONE, e.g., Eastern, Pacific]

Find the first available slot that matches my preferences
and book the appointment.

Add a note to the appointment: "[MEETING PURPOSE]"
```

## Example Output

After running this prompt, Claude might respond with:

> I checked your "Sales Calls" calendar for this week.
>
> Available slots matching your preferences (Tue-Thu,
> 1pm to 4pm Eastern):
>
> - Tuesday, April 8 at 1:30 PM
> - Wednesday, April 9 at 2:00 PM
> - Thursday, April 10 at 1:00 PM
>
> I booked the first available slot: **Tuesday, April 8 at
> 1:30 PM** for a 30-minute meeting with David Park.
>
> Note added: "Discovery call - discuss agency growth plan"

## Customizing the Prompt

**Just check availability.** If you do not want to book yet, just ask Claude to show open slots:

```
Check my GoHighLevel calendar [CALENDAR NAME] for available
30-minute slots this week. Show me all options between
9am and 5pm [TIMEZONE]. Do not book anything yet.
```

**Respond to a lead.** If a lead asked about booking, have Claude handle the reply:

```
The contact [CONTACT NAME] asked about scheduling a call.
Check my calendar [CALENDAR NAME] for the next 3 available
30-minute slots this week.

Draft a reply message offering those 3 time slots and
save it as a draft on the contact record.
```

**Reschedule an existing appointment.** Move a booking to a new time:

```
Find the appointment with [CONTACT NAME] on my
[CALENDAR NAME] calendar.

Reschedule it to the next available slot after
[DATE/TIME]. Add a note explaining the reschedule.
```

**Cancel an appointment.** Remove a booking cleanly:

```
Find and cancel the appointment with [CONTACT NAME] on
my [CALENDAR NAME] calendar.

Add a note to the contact record: "Appointment canceled
on [DATE]. Reason: [REASON]."
```

## Tips for Better Results

**Use the exact calendar name.** Check your GHL calendar settings for the precise name. Claude needs an exact match to find the right calendar.

**Specify the timezone every time.** This avoids confusion, especially if your GHL account and your contacts are in different time zones.

**Start with read-only tests.** Try "show me availability" before "book the appointment" to make sure Claude is reading the right calendar.
