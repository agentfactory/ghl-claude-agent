Use Case: Manage Your Pipeline

This prompt tells Claude to review your pipeline, find stalled deals, move them to the right stage, and give you a summary report.

Time saved: 15 to 30 minutes of pipeline cleanup, done in seconds.

What This Does

  1. Claude checks all deals in a specific pipeline stage
  2. Identifies which ones have not been updated recently
  3. Moves stalled deals to a "Needs Attention" stage
  4. Adds a note to each contact explaining the move
  5. Gives you a summary of everything that changed

The Prompt

Copy this prompt and paste it into Claude. Replace the placeholder values.

In my GoHighLevel account, look at the pipeline called
[YOUR PIPELINE NAME].

Find every opportunity in the [STAGE NAME] stage that has
not been updated in the last [NUMBER] days.

For each stalled opportunity:
1. Move it to the [TARGET STAGE NAME] stage
2. Add a note: "Moved by Claude - no activity for
   [NUMBER] days. Needs follow-up."

When you are done, give me a summary table with:
- Contact name
- Days since last activity
- Previous stage
- Any notes on the contact record

Example Output

After running this prompt, Claude might respond with:

I found 6 opportunities in the "Proposal Sent" stage of your Sales Pipeline. 3 of them have not been updated in over 14 days.

I moved them to "Needs Attention" and added notes.

Customizing the Prompt

Change the threshold. Use 3, 7, 14, or 30 days depending on how fast your sales cycle moves.

Change the pipeline. Replace the pipeline name with whichever pipeline you want to clean up.

Skip the move, just report. If you only want a status report without moving anything:

In my GoHighLevel account, look at the pipeline called
[YOUR PIPELINE NAME].

Give me a summary of every stage, showing how many
opportunities are in each and which contacts have not
been updated in the last 7 days.

Do not move anything. Just report.

Add follow-up actions. Ask Claude to also draft a follow-up message for each stalled contact:

For each stalled opportunity you move, also draft a
follow-up message to the contact. Save it as a draft.
Include the draft text in your summary table.

Weekly Pipeline Review

Set up a habit of running this prompt every Monday morning:

Give me a full pipeline review for [YOUR PIPELINE NAME].

For each stage, show me:
- How many opportunities are in this stage
- Total dollar value in this stage
- How many are stalled (no update in 7+ days)
- The top 3 highest-value opportunities

Then move any opportunity stalled for 14+ days to
[TARGET STAGE NAME] and add a note.

This gives you a complete pipeline snapshot in about 30 seconds.
